# L2-2 Entity·Concept Hub Guide

This guide defines the body structure of **L2-2 entity·concept hubs** (`wiki/entities/*.md`·`wiki/concepts/*.md`). For the common frontmatter and Root Meta Files Exception, see [layers/README.md](README.md); for slugs and file names, [naming.md](../policies/naming.md); for language conventions, [language.md](../policies/language.md); for other Layer page formats, use the "Layer classification" table in [layers/README.md](README.md) as the entry point.

## Body Structure

The body **must** include the two H2 sections below (auto-verified by `/wiki-lint hub body`).

### Required Sections

- **`## Overview`** (Overview) — the subject/concept's definition, position, and significance. The leading H2 of the file, 1–3 paragraphs of prose. No abstract summary — start with concrete facts, figures, and relations.
- **`## Connections`** (Connections) — a list of links to related hubs and sources within the wiki. The closing H2 of the file, in the form `- [[PageName]] — one-line relation description`. Citation type prefixes (`cites:`·`references:`, etc.) are not applied here — a hub is a navigational anchor, so it runs on plain wikilinks, and `tools/_build/graph.py` emits them as `references` relations by default. Each link target must have a supporting passage in the body (`## Overview` or an optional section); do not add a hub that never appears in the body to the connections only (dangling).

### Optional Sections

Place freely between the two required sections — a domain judgment. Hub bodies are in English, so the optional headings are English: `## Key Facts` (entity-general)·`## Definition`·`## Key Points` (concept-general)·`## Key Items`·`## Key Products`·`## Market Context`, etc.

### Minimum Stub Criteria

A body (frontmatter excluded) of total length **≥ 200 characters** + adherence to the two required sections + at least 1 link in `## Connections`.

### Authoring Acts — stub authoring vs full hub authoring

The two authoring acts of an L2-2 hub are distinguished not by the H2 count or body length of the output but by the **cycle they go through**.

- **stub authoring** — the Reporter (ingest fanout) or the Editor-in-Chief (post-fanout cross-source threshold judgment). The first creation of a new entity/concept, a fact listing based on one or a few sources. The output cap is the minimum stub criteria above plus 1–2 optional sections. It does not include cross-source synthesis prose, timeline narrative, or full rewriting of an existing hub. Desk qualitative review applies — limited to format, attribution, and narrative tone (the hub-stub-threshold judgment is the main-thread spot-check area).
- **full hub authoring** — the Columnist. Substantially expands or rewrites an existing hub through a deep cross-source read of many sources. Synthesis prose of facts, relations, and tensions + integrated timeline narrative. Same as L2-2 timeline, L2-3, and L2-4: GROUND·APPLY·ADAPT → Copy Editor quantitative VERIFY → Desk qualitative VERIFY chain.

A hub created by stub authoring may later be expanded by full hub authoring, at which point in its cycle it becomes subject to Desk review. A promotion candidate that is heavily cited (inbound) yet thin in the body is surfaced as a first-pass triage by `python tools/lint.py hub promotion` (stub ∩ sources≥5 ∩ inbound≥10 or cross-cluster≥3; strong = all three of multi-source, high citation, and broad distribution). Promotion is confirmed via a Desk second-pass gate (overlap with an adjacent hub's synthesis area · upward absorption into a concept · single-subject skew) and then proceeds to Columnist full hub authoring. A hub that has completed full authoring carries the frontmatter `gate: full` — since stub↔full is split by cycle (above), not body length, even a compact owner hub that has gone through the cycle is promotion-complete. The three terminal outcomes of `gate` are `full`·`delegated`·`absorbed`, all of which are excluded from promotion triage.

Conversely, a stub that has no independent navigational value — one-off source ∩ low citation ∩ single cluster — is surfaced by `python tools/lint.py hub demotion` as a candidate for plain-text demotion or deletion (sources≤2 ∩ inbound≤3 ∩ cluster≤1; strong = single-source ∩ inbound≤2 ∩ single cluster). Inbound is judged by kind, not count — if an overview, root, or another hub links to it (a navigational embed), it is not an orphan and so is excluded, and a hub-defining source, a theme register, or `_archive` inbound does not count as navigational. The threshold is the inverse of the lower bound that justifies a stub's existence (≥3 citations · multi-cluster · narrative-central), with naming.md's entity-addition threshold as the SoT. Demotion is confirmed via a Desk second-pass gate (narrative centrality · person significance) plus graph-integrity handling (on deletion, redirect inbound `[[wikilink]]`s · substitute with plain text) — gate-terminal hubs (including absorbed/delegated citation anchors) are excluded.

### Authoring craft (all hubs — skill-grounded)

A hub takes this page format, body structure, and the authoring craft below as its authoring standard, without a separate `## Authoring` or `## Evaluation Rubric` section. Applied to stub and full alike — **the principles and criteria's SoT is the dotted-ID craft skills**, and the below is the hub-application core. (A stub author writing from a one-off source is especially weak on Voice distancing, Attribution rigor, and Source consistency — a Desk VERIFY₂ pre-block area.)

- **Voice distancing** (`enc.verdict-restraint`·`cit.claimant-link`) — distance value-judgment assertions by naming the speaking subject or hedging. Frequent patterns: absorbing a person's voice · corporate PR · government self-designation · a vendor's own quantitative/completion-form assertions · internal frequency/historical/market-position/global-comparison assertions · source-unregistered superlatives (`largest`·`first`·`representative`·`core`) asserted.
- **Attribution rigor** (`cit.claimant-link`·`cit.speaker-link`) — attribute a source/speaking subject to quoted citations, assessments, rankings, and quantitative claims. Trade-media rankings ("N leaders"·"representative") get classifier attribution; no asymmetry of rules across H2 sections on one page.
- **Source consistency** (`cit.cite-consistency`) — downgrade an unregistered strong assertion to narrator generality, do not add titles/history/figures beyond the source's scope, and reflect the source's self-acknowledged limitation in at least one line. **Special rule for a new stub of a well-known entity**: limit product names, quantities, market position, and competitive landscape to what appears in the sources' bodies — no supplementary assertions of general knowledge outside the corpus (nationality · history · business model · product lineup; hedge if needed), list multi-source figures per source (no synthesis into a single range), no attribution of market-wide facts to a single entity, no cross-misattribution of an adjacent entity's products or facts.
- **Self-acknowledged label consistency** (`cit.*` verifiable attribution) — contrast a hedge label ("self-acknowledged limitation") against the strength of the quoted original; if the utterance is source-absent or mostly externally re-cited, rename it to "verification gap" or "no external assessment".
- **No self-meta voice** (`enc.encyclopedic-tone`) — no self-reference to editorial decisions. Do not write "this hub …", "this page …", "is organized separately", or "here we keep only ~" (no `--fix`). The rule applies to wikilink aliases and footnotes too. However, subject scoping of the form "it appears/is cited as ~ in this wiki" is not an antipattern (the recommended voice that situates a subject's role within the wiki via attribution/hedging) — what is forbidden is editorial-*decision* narrative ("we place it as ~"·"organize separately"). `python tools/lint.py hub voice` carries the literal antipattern regexes `본 hub는/의`·`본 페이지는/의`·`별도 정리한다`·`여기서는 ~만 유지한다` (Korean-corpus antipatterns; the lint's self-meta detection runs only under `WIKI_LANG=ko`).
- **English abbreviation gloss** (`enc.abbr-gloss`) — gloss once in parentheses at first occurrence (a person's name-origin gloss is not an acronym, so handle the meaning gloss separately). Raw-kept abbreviations (API, LLM, etc.) are an intentional, explicit list; if there are 5 or more, isolate the mapping in an HTML comment right after the frontmatter (keep the first-occurrence body gloss separately).
- **Body length·connection advisory** (`enc.nav-anchor-density`·`enc.connection-grouping`) — `python tools/lint.py hub body` detects prose ≥ 12,000 characters (sub-hub delegation) and `## Connections` flat links ≥ 50 (`### category` grouping). Prose excludes `## Connections`, frontmatter, and HTML comments, with an exemption for a central anchor of entity·inbound EXTRACTED ≥ 100; `### category` grouping is regardless of count (do not pass the threshold by deleting valid connections).

### Full hub structure·lifecycle

The hub-structure decisions for full hub authoring (Columnist deep cross-source). ①~③ apply the encyclopedic craft (`enc.summary-style` detail delegation · `enc.coatrack` tangential blocking) to the hub lifecycle; ④ is editorial integrity (not craft).

- **① Avoid timeline-narrative duplication** (`enc.summary-style`) — the hub body's `## Timeline` section is a date→event skeleton only; the detail, quotes, and diagnosis go in one topic section. Good: `**YYYY-MM-DD** — event (detail in the 「section」 section)` / forbidden: restating facts, figures, or quotes. `python tools/lint.py hub timeline` detects the item count (≥30 → split into a separate timeline page), strict ordering, and pointer ratio (<50% recommended). A separate standalone `wiki/timelines/<slug>.md` page is not pointer-form but **source-indexed** (each item is `[[source-id]]`-first) — [`timeline.md`](timeline.md) is the SoT. This section is limited to the in-hub summary section.
- **② entity-cluster absorption judgment** (`enc.summary-style`·`enc.coatrack`) — an entity full hub delegates the cluster-overview synthesis with "(market-side detail in [[<cluster-overview>]])". Absorption default: ⑴ a global single-domain big-tech product/subsidiary ⑵ a research/benchmark institution (citation anchor) ⑶ a bidirectional single-domain parent-of relation. When not promoted, the `gate`-attachment / promotion-triage-exclusion mechanism is in the "Authoring Acts" section above.
- **②′ person absorption judgment** (`kind: person`) — a person entity **defaults to absorption into the affiliated-organization hub**. If the title/activity overlaps with a single affiliated-organization hub (e.g., a company CEO ↔ that company's hub), the organization hub carries the narrative, the person stub is kept as a navigational anchor, and `gate: absorbed` is attached. **Full hub promotion is the exception** — only when **all** of ⑴ not reducible to a single organization (an independent actor spanning multiple organizations/issues) ∩ ⑵ inbound cross-cluster ≥ 3 ∩ ⑶ the individual's activity/views are themselves the narrative mainstay (not substitutable by the affiliated organization's facts) are satisfied. A one-off (≤2 source) person is plain-text demoted (naming.md entity-addition threshold). The `kind` identification and threshold branching are auto-applied by `python tools/lint.py hub promotion`.
- **③ shared-owner delegation** (`enc.summary-style`) — an event/mechanism shared by two or more hubs is narrated by one owner, with the rest as delegation pointers (mutually stating the role split in the overview). Confirm the delegation target actually holds the detail (no dangling).
- **④ wiki-operator COI disclosure** (editorial integrity — not craft; cf. WP:COI · journalistic disclosure) — when the operator is the source author or speaking subject, explicitly disclose "analysis conducted by the wiki operator". Disclose independently on each cross-cited hub.

**self-VERIFY₀** — after authoring, confirm `hub voice`·`hub timeline`·`hub body` PASS. After ≤ 2 self-attempts on the same cause, either PASS or hand off (SoT: [`agents/columnist.md`](../agents/columnist.md)).

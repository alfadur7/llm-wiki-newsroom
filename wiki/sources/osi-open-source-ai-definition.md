## Summary

The Open Source Initiative's reference page for the Open Source AI Definition (OSAID) 1.0 sets out what makes an AI system open source: the freedoms to use, study, modify, and share it for any purpose without permission, conditioned on access to the data information, code, and parameters needed to recreate a substantially equivalent system. It frames the definition as necessary because traditional software licenses do not cover AI's extra components, and lists the models that passed validation.

## Key Claims

- [fact] [[OpenSourceInitiative]] — defines an [[OpenSourceAI]] system by four freedoms (use, study, modify, share) plus required access to data information, code, and parameters
- [fact] [[OpenSourceInitiative]] — validated Pythia, OLMo, Amber, CrystalCoder, and T5 as OSAID-compliant, and judged [[Meta]]'s Llama 2 non-compliant
- [analysis] [[OpenSourceInitiative]] — argues a binary definition is needed to inform regulators and to combat [[OpenWashing]]
- [analysis] [[OpenSourceInitiative]] — treats [[OpenWeights]] models as a separate category that does not satisfy the full freedoms

## Key Quotes

> "Companies are calling AI systems 'Open Source' even though their licenses contain restrictions that go against the accepted principles and freedoms of Open Source." — [[OpenSourceInitiative]]

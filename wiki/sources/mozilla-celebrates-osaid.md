## Summary

In a post by Ayah Bdeir, Imo Udom, and Nik Marda, Mozilla endorses the Open Source Initiative's new Open Source AI Definition as an important step forward. It argues a shared, binary definition brings clarity for developers, advocates, and regulators and helps combat open-washing, while conceding that the definition's treatment of training data is imperfect and will need refinement over time.

## Key Claims

- [fact] [[Mozilla]] — endorses the [[OpenSourceInitiative]]'s Open Source AI Definition as "an important step forward"
- [analysis] [[Mozilla]] — argues a binary definition gives developers, advocates, and regulators needed precision and helps fight [[OpenWashing]]
- [analysis] [[Mozilla]] — characterizes "open-ish models like Meta's Llama 3" as the kind of release the definition is meant to clarify
- [forecast] [[Mozilla]] — intends to make open datasets a more commonplace part of the AI ecosystem and will publish supporting norms

## Key Quotes

> "Mozilla is excited about today's new definition of open source AI, and we endorse it as an important step forward." — [[Mozilla]]

## Overview

The Open Source Initiative (OSI) is the nonprofit, formed in 1998, that has stewarded the Open Source Definition for software and, in 2024-10, released the Open Source AI Definition (OSAID) 1.0. The OSAID was produced through a multi-year co-design process and defines an [[OpenSourceAI]] system by four freedoms plus required access to data information, code, and parameters. OSI validated a short list of compliant models (Pythia, OLMo, Amber, CrystalCoder, T5) and judged [[Meta]]'s Llama 2 non-compliant. The definition was endorsed by at least 20 organizations including [[Mozilla]], but drew criticism over its treatment of [[TrainingData]] — including from OSI co-founder Bruce Perens — and was approved by OSI's 10-person board rather than its full membership.
